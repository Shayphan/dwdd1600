Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the ability to use technology responsibly, safely and respectfully. It refers to the ability to protect private information online, mitigate risks associated with cyberthreats or online threats, and utilize information and media in a respectful, knowledgeable and legal way.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law is defined as electronic responsibility for actions and deeds. In other words, digital law refers to what you are and are not allowed to do while using the Internet.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy is a document which addresses all rights, privileges, responsibilities and sanctions associated with the use of the internet and digital technologies within an organization like work or school.

What is plagiarism?

the act of using another person's words, ideas, images, or work without proper attribution, passing them off as one's own.

It doesn't matter if it's done on purpose or accidentally, it's still plagiarism.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty refers to committing or contributing to dishonest acts by those engaged in teaching, learning, research, and related academic activities, and it applies not just to students, but to everyone in the academic environment

In other words, it is cheating, plagiarism, fabrication or falsification, and sabotage

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

Ai generated content may only be used responsibly as a tool to support your work and or learning. It is not a replacement for thinking or original work.
